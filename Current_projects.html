PREDICARE. Prediction and Control of Cardiovascular Events using Gradual and Delayed Correlations. Principal investigator. The main goal of the project is to extract from biometric signal recordings the rich information contained in the multi-step correlations between gradual changes of health parameters and past cardiovascular events, which are mostly manifested in small changes with variable time delays, such as the gradual effects of taking a medicine or following a diet. We propose a method that extracts these gradual and delayed correlations (GDCs) and encodes them into dynamics models (action rules) grounded on a hybrid representation of continuous and discrete (symbolic) variables. Discrete variables are used to encode the dynamics of the health of patients using medical terms, which permits a direct interpretation of the results by clinicians. This discrete representation is fully compatible with advanced artificial intelligence techniques for symbolic planning, which permits generating alternative plans (treatments) to maximize the well-being of patients in the long run. The continuous variable representation, on the other hand, is used to ground the medical terms in mathematical parameters obtained from heart rate variability analysis, whose evolution is correlated with the one of medical terms for a better prediction of the health state evolution. These techniques are integrated into an Internet platform for the online monitoring of cardiovascular patients using wearable technology.

UNIPHYED. Task planning for autonomous robots based on the unification of symbols and physics. Principal investigator. The usual approach to implement a robotic platform capable of executing human-like tasks is to combine methods from artificial intelligence with robotic mechanisms using different representations and search techniques. This makes integration and consistency checking between techniques very complicated, preventing the accurate predictions of the consequences of actions in real scenarios and, hence, the generation of successful plans. The main claim of this project is that, in order to reliably and accurately predict the consequences of robotic actions, the symbolic descriptions needed by artificial intelligence methods for making decisions should be evaluated directly from the continuous variables of the physical phenomena they represent. We propose a unified representation that permits this evaluation. In particular, we associate to predicates and planning operators (POs) probability density models representing the physical instances where the evaluations were positive or negative (e.g. true and false values of predicates or success or failure probabilities of POs). Using the unified representation, we define a probabilistic planning approach which uses these enhanced prediction capabilities to generate reliable plans for the execution of human-like tasks in real robotic scenarios.

ECHO0 (2018). Efficient Multi-goal Decision-making using Spiking Neural Networks. Development of a biologically inspired decision-making framework based on Spiking neural Networks (SNNs) that combines features of reactive and deliberative decision-making paradigms. For each task, the system is able to quickly select an action for a given situation without the need of deliberation, as in reactive approaches, and, if a new task is required, to change the action policy without the need of re-learning, as in multi-goal deliberative approaches. Perception, decision-making, and action mechanisms are structured in three SNNs modules with strong inhibitory components. The approach is implemented using liquid state machine networks and it is based on an inside-out perception mechanisms regulated by internal goals.

PLANTAR. Automatic control of the growth of plants using artificial intelligence and Internet technology. The project concerns the development of a centralized monitoring system that integrates advanced artificial intelligence (AI) techniques of machine learning and decision-making with Internet technology for the automatic monitoring and control of the growth of plants in large horticultural crops. The system automatically decides the treatment to apply to each plant according to the plant’s individual needs rather than establishing general protocols for nutrient, water, and light/shading common to all the plants, as done traditionally. This permits reducing the resources needed for an optimal crop yield, making a more sustainable management of water and nutrients while reducing costs. The artificial intelligence techniques are based on a novel machine learning mechanisms that is able to find the correlations between gradual and small changes observed on plants with past events taking care on the plant  (e.g. the gradual and delayed growth of leaves produced by past supplies of water and nutrient). The AI mechanisms are integrated into an Internet platform that permits an expert gardener to supervise the artificial intelligence system as well as the treatments applied to the plants at any time and from any place through suitable web interfaces.
