The HRV2 is a platform for the permanent monitoring of ambulatory cardiovascular patients. The monitoring is carried out from parameters derived from biomedical signals, such as heart rate and level of activity, obtained from commercially available wearable devises (e.g. smartwatches). These parameters are used by an artificial intelligence inference engine that estimates the acute risk of the patient of having a severe cardiovascular event.


The HRV2 platform connects patients with clinicians through suitable graphical user interfaces (GUIs). Each clinician is able to monitor a group of patients through a specially designed web-interface that permits selecting a patient and deploying his/her history of health states at any time and from any place (with Internet access). The platform also sends simple alerts to patients, a responsible relative of the patient, and the corresponding clinician in case a high acute risk is detected.


The platform provides the possibility of creating a real-life patient, which will be monitored through wearable technology, or a simulated patient, which is used to generate surrogate data.
